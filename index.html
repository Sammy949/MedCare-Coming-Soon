Monkeypox

* Information about Monkeypox *

Monkey-pox is a rare but potentially serious disease that is caused by the monkey-pox virus. Monkey-pox virus is from the same family of viruses as the smallpox virus. It is also less severe and transmissible than smallpox. Monkey-pox can spread from infected humans, animals, and materials contaminated with the virus. Monkey-pox virus is characterized by a new, unexplained rash and skin lesions. It is usually found in Central and West Africa and normally does not spread in the United States...More

Tips

Wash your Hands

You should wash your hands properly with soap and clean running water to reduce the spread of the Coronavirus

To Protect your self from the Coronavirus and Its Variants

Get Vaccinated, Use an alcohol-based sanitizer and always wear your face mask when in the public.

Social Distance

Maintain a safe distance from anyone who is coughing or sneezing. Always maintain a social distance of at least 6m long to prevent the spread of Coronavirus when in the public.

Touching of the face

Avoid unnecessary touching your eyes, nose or mouth as you may have touched an infected surface with your hands.

Sneezing

Cover your nose and mouth with a tissue when coughing or sneezing and if you do not have a tissue with you, cover your nose and mouth with your bent elbow.

Stay At Home

If you are feeling unwell, stay home until yo feel better. If you ave a fever, cough and difficulty breathing, seek medical attention. Call in advance and follow the directions of your local health authority.

Avoid unnecessary visits

Avoiding unneeded/unnecessary visits to medical facilities allows the healthcare systems to operate more effectively, therefore protecting you and others.

Self-Care

If you feel sick you should rest, drink plenty of fluid, and eat nutritious food. Stay in a separate room from other familiy members, and use a dedicated bathroom if possible. Clean and disinfect frequently touched surfaces.
